This Conversation Actually Explains Oneness With The Universe

On this podcast episode, our co-founder Dan Harris speaks with Sebene Selassie about the concept of belonging, "oneness with the universe," and what it means to be different, but not separate.

LISTEN TO THE PODCAST

We all know that belonging—to a tribe, a family, a group of any sort—is a key part of human happiness; science bears this out.


Sebene Selassie, a writer and teacher based in Brooklyn and a regular on the Ten Percent Happier app, is taking this concept of belonging to a much, much deeper level. To the “oneness with the universe” level. That’s obviously one of the world’s greatest spiritual cliches, but in her new book, You Belong, Sebene unpacks and defends the concept incredibly effectively.


This week, we're highlighting a podcast conversation between Sebene and our co-founder, Dan Harris, as well as new wisdom from Sebene on the Ten Percent Happier app, directly inspired by her work on belonging.

What ‘Belonging’ Really Means
by Sebene Selassie


 

For most of my life, I longed to belong. I longed to fit in, I longed to achieve success, and I longed to have a soul mate. And yet, also for most of my life I felt I did not belong anywhere.


I was a toddler when my family emigrated from Ethiopia in the early seventies. I felt out of place in an American culture that was a lot less diverse than it is today, and in an immigrant community that was much smaller than it is now. I grew up Black in white neighborhoods, and I didn’t feel like I connected to any one racial culture. I was a girl who was not interested in girlie things.
